Frequently Asked Any Questions

Get quick answers to commonly asked legal questions. Our FAQs cover a wide range of topics to help you better understand your rights and options.

The statute of limitations is the time period within which a person must file a lawsuit. The time period varies depending on the type of case and the jurisdiction. It's important to be aware of the statute of limitations for your case so you don't miss the deadline to file.

Misdemeanors are less serious crimes that are punishable by a fine or a maximum of one year in jail. Felonies, on the other hand, are more serious crimes that are punishable by more than one year in prison. The specific penalties will vary depending on the jurisdiction and the specific crime.

If you are stopped by the police, you have the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney. You should not resist arrest, but you should assert your rights by stating that you wish to remain silent and that you wish to have an attorney present during any questioning.
